Girl Frisking Soldier —Banksy
A direct critique on Israeli over-the-top surveillance procedures that Palestinians are imposed to all through the West Bank.
Rage, the Flower Thrower —Banksy
Though dressed in riot gear, this man is ready to throw flowers instead of Molotov bombs. Located in Jerusalem, Banksy is advocating for peace instead of war as a two-state solution for Palestine and Israeli conflicts. The work also carries the message that peace comes with active hard work. This graffiti also goes to show the international presence and interest in the Israeli-Palestinian conflict.
The Walled Off Hotel —Banksy
Designed by Banksy, this hotel in Bethlehem encapsulates the environment of Palestinian abandoned/demolished houses as well as more universal scenes of war zones. This whole play of luxury and destruction cause by war is ironic and shows the extent of Banksy’s interest in the conflict.
Handala
Al Ali’s emblem, Handala, is the child witness of the refugee camps who gets his name from an Arabic word for a bitter plant called al-handal to represent the bitterness of the Palestinian experience. Handala turns his face from the gazer’s view in defiance of the international, historical forces opposing Palestinian liberation and of Arab complicity, lethargy and impotence.
"Make Hummus Not Walls"
A word play of the infamous phrase “Make Love Not War”. Though not ill-intentioned, this phrase gives a really clear, Western impression of the West Bank and Middle East in general.
Jaffa Oranges
The symbolism of the orange as a Palestinian nationalist symbol is complicated by the near parity achieved by Jewish cultivators toward the end of the Mandate period. Based on these pre-1948 statistics of land under cultivation, the orange, as an agricultural product, could logically serve as a fitting symbol for coexistence, a binational product befitting the sought-after binational state.
Leila Khaled
Leila Khaled’s actions short after the Six Day War have impacted on the way that Palestinians as a people have been viewed by the world for the past 35 years, and have ensured that she looms large in discussions of women, the Middle East, and the tactics of liberation struggles.
Hotel Parking
This sign is one of many traffic signals one might find attached to the wall. Since most roads run beside the wall, one might find signals and commercial advertisements on the Separation Wall.
Station 16
This is one of the many international companies that makes graffiti on the wall by commission as well as merchandise about mostly controversial topics. This example out of many shows the international artist fixation on the Palestinian-Israeli conflict not only to create and share a message, but also for profit.
“Women be safe”
Messages written on walls during the first intifada often served as warnings/reminders for fellow Palestinians. They could also direct people where to gather.
“The Intifada continues”
This was often written during the first intifada as a warning that would be there overnight and gone by dawn. This also talks about the continuing discontent of Palestinians in the West Bank.
“The Intifada”
The name of the violent protests of Palestinians against Israeli oppressing and segregating policies.
